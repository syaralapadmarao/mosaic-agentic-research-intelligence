# Management Meeting Note — Apollo Hospitals, Chennai HQ

**Date:** 5 December 2025
**Visited by:** Rohan Kapoor, Healthcare Analyst
**Type:** Management meeting (1-on-1)
**Participants:** Dr. Sangita Reddy (JMD), CFO (name withheld)

---

## Key Discussion Points

### HealthCo Breakeven Timeline
- Dr. Sangita Reddy was very direct: "HealthCo will break even by Q4 FY27. This is not aspirational, it's a commitment."
- Pharmacy segment is already EBITDA positive on a per-store basis when excluding new store opening costs. 200 stores opened in FY26 so far; plan is to moderate to 100/year going forward and focus on same-store growth.
- Apollo 24/7 digital platform: MAU at 8M but management acknowledged that monetization is "still early." The strategy is to use the app as a patient acquisition funnel for hospitals rather than a standalone profit centre. "Every ₹1 spent on 24/7 generates ₹4 of hospital revenue within 18 months through follow-up care."
- The biggest surprise: Management is exploring a B2B SaaS model for the Apollo 24/7 platform — licensing the technology to smaller hospital chains in Tier-2/3 cities and in Southeast Asia. This was not previously discussed publicly. "We've had inbound interest from 6 hospital chains. We're evaluating."

### Proton Therapy Centre
- The Chennai proton therapy centre is India's first and management sees it as a significant medical tourism catalyst.
- Investment of ₹400 Cr with expected payback of 5-6 years. Currently in testing phase, patient treatments expected to begin Q4 FY26.
- Management expects 60% of proton therapy patients to be international (primarily Middle East, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka) given the lack of facilities in the region.
- "We're already getting inquiries. We have 40+ patients pre-registered for treatment."

### Occupancy Trajectory
- Management acknowledged the occupancy gap vs peers (69% vs 75-77% for Max/Narayana).
- Attributed it to: (a) pan-India footprint including Tier-2 cities where ramp-up is slower, (b) several facilities in the 2-3 year ramp-up phase, (c) deliberate capacity reservation for emergency and medical tourism patients.
- "Our mature hospitals (>5 years old) run at 78-82% occupancy. The blended number is depressed by newer facilities."
- Expects blended occupancy to cross 72% by FY28 as newer facilities mature.

### Capital Allocation
- Cash on books: ~₹2,000 Cr. Management is "actively evaluating" 2-3 acquisition targets but would not provide details.
- "We're disciplined acquirers. We won't pay more than 12x EBITDA for any acquisition."
- Dividend policy: management indicated willingness to increase payout ratio from current 15% to 20-25% "once HealthCo is self-funding."

### Competitive View
- On Max Healthcare: "They've done a great job in NCR. But their model is a Delhi story. We're a national story."
- On Narayana Health: "Different segment. They're value-healthcare. We're premium-and-tertiary."
- On medical tourism competition from Thailand/Turkey: "India's cost advantage is 60-70% vs Thailand for comparable outcomes. The gap is widening, not narrowing."

---

## My Assessment

Management is confident and articulate, as always with Apollo. The HealthCo breakeven commitment is the most important thing I heard — if they deliver, it removes the biggest narrative overhang on the stock. The B2B SaaS angle for Apollo 24/7 is new information and could be a meaningful optionality that the market isn't pricing.

However, I remain cautious on two fronts:
1. The acquisition pipeline makes me nervous. Apollo's historical M&A track record is mixed — the 2015 Sindh Hospital acquisition in Mumbai was a value destroyer.
2. The occupancy excuse ("mature facilities are at 78-82%") is mathematically correct but doesn't change the fact that consolidated returns are diluted by sub-scale newer facilities.

**Conviction: Neutral-to-positive.** The HealthCo breakeven and proton therapy are genuine catalysts, but I want to see actual evidence before re-rating.
