## Investment Thesis — India's Hospital Platform Play

Apollo Hospitals reported Q2 FY26 revenue of ₹6,319 Cr — a record quarter driven by hospital revenue growth of 16% YoY and continued momentum in Apollo HealthCo (pharmacy + digital). EBITDA at ₹941 Cr (+22% YoY) demonstrates the operating leverage inherent in Apollo's asset-heavy model as occupancy improves. We reiterate our Conviction List BUY with a target price of ₹7,800.

Apollo's competitive moat is widening. With 8,050 operational beds across 73 hospitals, 5,800+ pharmacies, and a digital platform with 35M+ registered users, no other Indian healthcare company offers this level of vertical integration. The Q2 results confirm our thesis that Apollo is transitioning from a hospital chain to a healthcare platform — and the market is still valuing it as the former.

## Key Highlights

### Hospital Business
- Hospital revenue at ₹4,180 Cr (+16% YoY), driven by higher surgical volumes and improving case mix
- Occupancy at 69% — up 200 bps YoY but still significantly below the 75-78% levels of NCR-focused peers, reflecting Apollo's pan-India footprint including Tier-2/3 markets where ramp-up takes longer
- ARPOB at ₹1,733K — highest in the industry, reflecting Apollo's positioning as the premium brand in multi-specialty care
- Bed capacity at 9,483 with 8,050 operational — the gap between total and operational capacity represents a natural growth runway requiring minimal additional capex
- EBITDA per Bed at ₹1.86 Lakhs — this metric needs to be viewed in context of Apollo's larger bed base and pan-India presence which includes sub-scale facilities in smaller markets

### Apollo HealthCo
- Pharmacy network at 5,800+ stores — added 120 stores in Q2
- Digital platform revenue growing at 40%+ YoY, though still a small contributor to consolidated numbers
- Apollo 24/7 app monthly active users crossed 8M — engagement metrics (session time, repeat visits) trending positively
- Management guided for HealthCo breakeven by Q4 FY27 — this would be a significant re-rating catalyst

### Expansion & Capex
- 3 new hospitals announced in Tier-2 cities (Vizag expansion, Pune greenfield, Lucknow)
- Total capex guidance of ₹2,500 Cr for FY26-FY27, funded primarily from internal accruals
- Proton therapy centre at Chennai — India's first, expected to drive medical tourism volumes

## Valuation

Apollo trades at 32x FY27E EV/EBITDA — a premium to the hospital sector. We believe this is justified by:
- **Pan-India franchise** — only hospital chain with meaningful presence across all top-15 cities
- **HealthCo optionality** — pharmacy + digital platform not fully valued by the market
- **ARPOB leadership** — reflects brand premium and complex case mix (cardiac, oncology, transplant)
- **Medical tourism moat** — 18% of Apollo's surgical revenue comes from international patients, a segment growing at 25%+ and largely insulated from domestic competitive pressures

We value Apollo on a sum-of-parts basis:
- Hospital business: 28x FY27E EBITDA = ₹6,200/share
- HealthCo (pharmacy + digital): 3x FY27E EV/Sales = ₹1,600/share
- **Total: ₹7,800/share**

## Risks

1. **HealthCo losses persist** — if the pharmacy/digital business doesn't achieve breakeven by Q4 FY27, the market may lose patience and the conglomerate discount could widen
2. **Regulatory intervention on pharmacy margins** — any capping of pharmacy margins (currently 20-25%) would directly impact Apollo given its 5,800-store network
3. **Pan-India expansion dilutes returns** — Apollo's entry into Tier-2/3 cities carries structurally lower returns than mature metros; if the proportion of sub-scale hospitals grows, blended ROCE could decline
4. **Key man risk** — the Reddy family's operational involvement is critical; any succession disruption could impact execution
5. **Medical tourism disruption** — geopolitical events or competing medical tourism destinations (Thailand, Turkey) could impact the 18% international surgery contribution
