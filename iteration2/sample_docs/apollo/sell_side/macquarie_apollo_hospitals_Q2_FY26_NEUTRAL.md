## Summary — Hospital Engine Firing, HealthCo Still a Drag

Apollo's Q2 FY26 was a tale of two businesses. The hospital segment delivered strongly with 16% YoY revenue growth and improving occupancy. However, the consolidated story is muddied by continued losses in Apollo HealthCo (pharmacy + digital), which depressed consolidated EBITDA margins to 14.9% — significantly below pure-play hospital peers like Max Healthcare (25.8%) and Narayana Health (20%+).

We maintain NEUTRAL. The hospital business alone would warrant a BUY, but the HealthCo overhang, combined with a rich valuation at 32x FY27E EV/EBITDA, limits near-term upside.

---

## Hospital Business — No Complaints

The hospital segment continues to impress:
- Revenue of ₹4,180 Cr represents 16% YoY growth — the strongest in the sector
- Occupancy at 69% has meaningful room to improve (peer average ~75%)
- ARPOB at ₹1,733K is industry-leading, supported by a surgical case mix that skews toward high-acuity procedures (cardiac surgery, organ transplants, complex oncology)
- Management's proton therapy centre in Chennai is a strategic differentiator for medical tourism

However, we note that EBITDA at ₹941 Cr on a consolidated basis implies a margin of only 14.9%. The hospital-only margin is closer to 22-23%, which is respectable but not exceptional given Apollo's premium ARPOB.

---

## HealthCo — The Elephant in the Room

Apollo HealthCo (pharmacy retail + Apollo 24/7 digital platform) remains a value drag:
- Combined revenue of ~₹2,100 Cr in Q2 but EBITDA losses of ~₹80-100 Cr
- Management's guided breakeven by Q4 FY27 requires a significant acceleration in same-store sales growth and digital monetization
- Pharmacy margins are structurally capped at 4-5% (net of rental costs) — this is a volume business, not a margin business
- The digital health platform (Apollo 24/7) has 8M MAUs but monetization per user remains negligible at ~₹15/month

We model HealthCo breakeven only in FY28 — 4 quarters behind management guidance. If we're right, the conglomerate discount will persist longer than bulls expect.

## Valuation & Target Price

We value Apollo at 26x FY27E consolidated EV/EBITDA, a 15% discount to the Conviction List bulls' 32x. Our ₹6,600 target implies ~2% upside — not enough to justify a BUY.

**Catalysts to upgrade:**
- HealthCo achieves quarterly EBITDA breakeven (would remove the conglomerate discount narrative)
- Occupancy crosses 72% on a sustained basis (would validate capacity utilization thesis)
- Pharmacy store economics inflect (same-store EBITDA turns positive after adjusting for rent)

**Catalysts to downgrade:**
- HealthCo losses widen beyond ₹400 Cr annually
- Hospital occupancy stagnates at sub-70%
- Management announces large M&A (Apollo has historically overpaid for acquisitions)
