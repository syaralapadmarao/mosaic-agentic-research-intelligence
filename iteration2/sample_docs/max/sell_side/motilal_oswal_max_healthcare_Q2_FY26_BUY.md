## Investment Thesis — Upgrade to BUY

Max Healthcare delivered a strong Q2 FY26 with revenue of ₹2,692 Cr (+10.8% YoY, +4.6% QoQ), ahead of our estimate of ₹2,580 Cr. EBITDA at ₹694 Cr implies a margin of ~25.8%, a meaningful expansion from 25.2% in Q1 FY26. We upgrade our target price to ₹1,150 on the back of accelerating bed additions and improving occupancy trajectory.

The key positive surprise was the 100 bps QoQ improvement in occupancy to 77%, driven by ramp-up at recently commissioned facilities. With ~1,500 beds in the commissioning pipeline over the next 12 months (Max Smart Saket 400 beds, Nanavati Phase 1 268 beds, Sector 56 Gurgaon 500 beds, Dehradun 130 beds), we see a clear pathway to sustained double-digit revenue growth through FY28.

## Key Highlights

### Revenue & Profitability
- Revenue at ₹2,692 Cr (+10.8% YoY) was 4.3% above our estimate, driven by higher ARPOB and improving occupancy
- EBITDA at ₹694 Cr represents a margin of 25.8%, up from 25.2% in Q1 FY26
- ARPOB remained stable at ₹77.3K, reflecting a healthy case mix tilted toward complex procedures
- EBITDA per Bed at ₹73.4 Lakhs (annualized) — among the highest in the sector

### Capacity & Occupancy
- Operational beds at 4,760 — up 106 beds QoQ as Max Lucknow expansion came online
- Occupancy at 77% (+100 bps QoQ) — encouraging given the typical Q2 seasonality softness
- Total bed capacity at 4,760 beds with pipeline of ~1,500 additional beds over 12 months
- Brownfield expansions at Dwarka and Patparganj are being planned to address projected capacity constraints

### Expansion Pipeline
- **Max Smart, Saket Complex** — 400 beds, commissioning expected Q3 FY26. This is the highest-margin facility in the network and the expansion will materially improve blended margins.
- **Nanavati (Mumbai)** — Phase 1 of 268 beds on track for completion by end of current fiscal. Mumbai market offers significant pricing power.
- **Sector 56, Gurgaon** — 500-bed greenfield, expected by end of FY27. This addresses growing demand in the NCR corridor.
- **Dehradun** — 130-bed specialty hospital (oncology + cardiac), commissioning by end of FY26.
- **Max Nagpur** — 100 beds, expected completion within 24 months. Entry into Central India market.

## Risks

1. **Execution risk on simultaneous multi-site commissioning** — 5 sites ramping simultaneously could stretch management bandwidth and temporarily depress blended margins
2. **Regulatory risk** — NITI Aayog discussions on hospital pricing caps remain a sector overhang; any capping framework would disproportionately impact premium operators like Max
3. **Occupancy ramp slower than expected** — Greenfield facilities (Gurgaon, Nagpur) typically take 18-24 months to reach 60%+ occupancy; delays would impact our FY28 estimates
4. **Competition from Fortis and Medanta in NCR** — capacity additions by competitors could put pressure on ARPOB growth in the Delhi-NCR market
5. **Working capital cycle elongation** — insurance mix shift toward government schemes could lengthen receivable days
