## Summary — Good Numbers, Rich Valuations

Max Healthcare's Q2 FY26 numbers were undeniably strong — revenue of ₹2,692 Cr and EBITDA of ₹694 Cr both beat consensus. However, at 26x forward EV/EBITDA, we believe the stock already discounts the visible bed addition pipeline and margin expansion story. We maintain our HOLD rating.

The occupancy improvement to 77% is noteworthy, but we flag that this was partly driven by seasonal elective surgery demand in Q2. The sustainability of this level needs verification over Q3-Q4 when elective volumes typically soften. More importantly, the simultaneous commissioning of ~1,500 beds across 5 sites creates near-term margin dilution risk that the market is not pricing in.

---

## What We Liked

- **Revenue beat** of 4.3% vs consensus driven by occupancy improvement and stable ARPOB
- **EBITDA margin expansion** to 25.8% reflects operating leverage and favorable case mix
- **Bed additions on track** — management's 92% delivery rate on guided timelines is best-in-class
- **Dehradun and Nanavati** commissioning timelines appear firm based on our site visit feedback

## What Concerns Us

- **Margin dilution ahead**: With 1,500 beds commissioning over 12 months, pre-operative costs will weigh on margins through FY27. Our model shows 150-200 bps of margin headwind from new facility ramp-up costs.
- **ARPOB stagnation**: ARPOB has been flat at ₹77-78K for three consecutive quarters. Management attributes this to case mix, but we see structural resistance from insurance company pushback on procedure pricing.
- **NCR competitive intensity**: Fortis's Gurugram expansion (350 beds, Q1 FY27 commissioning) and Medanta's greenfield in Noida will add ~700 beds to the NCR market by mid-FY27. This could pressure both occupancy and pricing.
- **Greenfield execution risk**: The 500-bed Sector 56 Gurgaon facility is Max's first large greenfield in 5+ years. Greenfield economics are meaningfully different from brownfield — we model 60% occupancy by Year 2 vs 85%+ for brownfield expansions.
- **Occupancy Rate at 77% may have seasonal benefit** — Q2 (Jul-Sep) typically sees higher elective volumes. We need Q3-Q4 confirmation before extrapolating the occupancy trend.

## Valuation

At CMP of ₹920, Max trades at 26x FY27E EV/EBITDA — a 30% premium to the hospital sector average. While we acknowledge the quality premium, the valuation leaves limited margin of safety.

Our target price of ₹980 is based on 24x FY27E EV/EBITDA, which we believe is fair for a company facing near-term margin dilution from aggressive capacity expansion.

**We would become more constructive at ₹800-850 levels**, which would imply 20x FY27E EV/EBITDA and provide adequate margin of safety for the execution risks ahead.

## Risks to Our View

**Upside risks:**
- Faster-than-expected occupancy ramp at new facilities (every 100 bps of occupancy adds ~₹25 Cr to annual EBITDA)
- ARPOB re-acceleration from complex surgery case mix shift
- Inorganic growth — Max has ₹2,000 Cr of cash on books and management has indicated interest in asset-light management contracts

**Downside risks:**
- Government pricing regulation (probability low but impact high)
- Key management departures — CEO Abhay Soi's strategic vision is central to the execution story
