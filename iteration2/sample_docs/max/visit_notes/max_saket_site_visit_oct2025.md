# Site Visit Note — Max Super Speciality Hospital, Saket

**Date:** 18 October 2025
**Visited by:** Rohan Kapoor, Healthcare Analyst
**Type:** Facility tour + management meeting
**Participants:** Dr. Sandeep Budhiraja (Group Medical Director), Yogesh Sareen (CFO)

---

## Key Observations

### Saket Complex Expansion (Max Smart — 400 beds)
- Physically visited the Max Smart construction site adjacent to the existing Saket campus. Structure appears 85-90% complete. Interior fit-out is underway on floors 3-8.
- CFO confirmed commissioning on track for Q3 FY26 (Dec-Jan timeframe). Phase 1 will be 200 beds, Phase 2 another 200 beds by end of Q3.
- Management very bullish on Saket expansion — they expect this to be the highest-margin facility in the network within 18 months of full commissioning due to shared infrastructure with existing campus (common kitchen, laundry, biomedical waste handling, power backup).
- Interesting insight: Max is designing the new facility with modular ICU pods that can be reconfigured between adult and pediatric use based on demand. This suggests they may be eyeing the pediatric segment more seriously.

### Occupancy & Demand Trends
- Dr. Budhiraja mentioned that their Delhi facilities (Saket, Patparganj, Dwarka) are running at 82-85% effective occupancy in peak months — the reported 77% includes the smaller ramp-up facilities which dilute the blended number.
- Wait times for elective cardiac surgery at Saket have increased to 10-12 days from 5-7 days a year ago. This confirms genuine capacity constraints.
- International patient volumes are growing ~15% YoY, primarily from the Middle East, Bangladesh, and Central Asia. Management is investing in a dedicated international patient lounge at the new facility.

### Competitive Positioning
- Asked about Fortis's Gurugram expansion. Response was measured — management acknowledged the capacity addition but noted that "the NCR market absorbs 2,000+ beds annually" and that their brand premium allows them to maintain pricing even in a competitive market.
- Management is watching Medanta's Noida greenfield closely but believes their multi-site network (5 hospitals in Delhi-NCR) provides convenience advantages that a single-site operator cannot match.

### Talent & Staffing
- Hiring for the Max Smart expansion is ~60% complete. Senior consultant recruitment is done; they are now filling junior doctor and nursing positions.
- CFO mentioned that staff costs are the single largest margin risk — nursing salaries have increased 12-15% YoY due to competition from Gulf healthcare employers. They are addressing this through a nursing academy partnership that will produce 200 graduates/year exclusively for Max facilities.

---

## My Assessment

The Saket expansion looks genuinely on track and the shared-infrastructure model should deliver superior unit economics vs standalone greenfield. The key risk is whether they can fill 400 beds fast enough — my estimate is 12-15 months to 70% occupancy based on the existing Saket brand pull. Management's confidence seems well-founded given the capacity constraints they're already facing.

The bigger question is whether the Sector 56 Gurgaon greenfield (500 beds) will replicate this success. Unlike Saket, that's a standalone facility in a new micro-market competing directly against Fortis and Medanta. I want to visit that site when construction progresses further.

**Conviction: Incrementally positive.** Confirms the near-term execution story. Longer-term NCR competitive dynamics remain the key debate.
