# Channel Check — Insurance Company Feedback on Max Healthcare

**Date:** 8 November 2025
**Conducted by:** Rohan Kapoor, Healthcare Analyst
**Type:** Phone calls with 3 insurance company executives
**Contacts:** Claims Head at Star Health, VP Medical at ICICI Lombard, Chief Underwriter at Niva Bupa

---

## Summary

Conducted channel checks with three major health insurers to assess Max Healthcare's negotiating position and pricing trends. The feedback was mixed — Max commands respect for clinical quality but insurers are increasingly pushing back on pricing.

---

## Star Health (Claims Head)

- Max is their #2 provider by claim value in North India after Fortis.
- "Max's pricing is 15-20% above market average for the same procedures. We've been in negotiation for 6 months to bring it down."
- Pre-authorization rejection rate for Max has increased from 8% to 12% over the past year — they are scrutinizing length-of-stay more aggressively.
- Interesting: Star Health is considering a preferred provider network that would exclude hospitals above a pricing threshold. Max would need to offer 10% discounts to remain in-network. "We haven't communicated this to Max yet."
- Star Health's claim data shows Max's patient outcomes (re-admission rates, infection rates) are among the best — "we don't dispute quality, we dispute pricing."

## ICICI Lombard (VP Medical)

- More positive on Max. "They're our most predictable partner. Clean documentation, fast discharge summaries, fewer claim disputes."
- ICICI Lombard is actually increasing its Max allocation — routing more cashless approvals to Max because "fewer fraudulent claims and better post-discharge compliance."
- Confirmed that Max's ARPOB premium is sustainable for complex cases (cardiac, onco, transplant) but under pressure for routine procedures (general surgery, orthopedics).
- "The real margin pressure will come from government schemes. If Ayushman Bharat expands to cover the ₹5L-15L income bracket, Max will face a choice: participate at government rates or lose 15-20% of their addressable market."

## Niva Bupa (Chief Underwriter)

- Niva Bupa has a relatively small Max Healthcare book but growing.
- "Max's documentation quality makes them easy to work with from an underwriting perspective."
- Flagged concern about Max's average length of stay being 0.5 days higher than industry average for comparable procedures. "This could be genuine complexity or it could be upcoding. We're investigating."
- Niva Bupa is building their own hospital quality scorecards and Max ranks top-3 in North India consistently.

---

## My Takeaways

1. **ARPOB resilience is real but not infinite.** Max can sustain premiums for complex cases, but routine procedure pricing is under pressure. This is consistent with the flat ARPOB trend we've seen over the last 3 quarters.

2. **Star Health's potential preferred-provider move is a risk to monitor.** If Star executes on a pricing threshold, Max would face a choice between margin compression and volume loss. Star Health covers ~15% of Max's insured patient base.

3. **Government scheme expansion is the medium-term structural risk.** All three insurers flagged Ayushman Bharat expansion as the key uncertainty. This is not yet in consensus models.

4. **Quality reputation is a genuine moat.** All three insurers acknowledged Max's superior clinical outcomes. This gives Max more pricing power than competitors, even if that power has limits.

**Net assessment: Neutral.** The quality moat is real but the pricing ceiling is lower than management suggests. I'm reducing my ARPOB growth assumption from 3% to 1.5% for FY27.
